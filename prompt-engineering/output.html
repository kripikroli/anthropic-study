# One-Day Meal Plan for Marathon Training Athlete

Here is a one-day meal plan for an athlete training for a marathon with high carbohydrate intake and proper meal timing around a 2-hour morning run:

* **Calorie Target:** Approximately 3,200 calories
* **Macronutrient Breakdown:** Protein (110g), Fat (85g), Carbs (450g)

## Meal Plan:

* **Pre-Run Breakfast (6:00 AM - 30 minutes before run):** Banana (150g) with coconut oil spread (15g) on white bread (50g). Orange juice (250ml).
    * Protein: 4g, Fat: 8g, Carbs: 65g

* **Post-Run Recovery Meal (8:30 AM - within 30 minutes of finishing):** White rice (150g cooked) with grilled chicken breast (120g), steamed carrots (100g), and olive oil (10ml).
    * Protein: 35g, Fat: 10g, Carbs: 60g

* **Mid-Morning Snack (11:00 AM):** Rice cakes (40g) with strawberry jam (30g) and honey (15g). Sports drink (300ml).
    * Protein: 3g, Fat: 2g, Carbs: 65g

* **Lunch (1:00 PM):** Turkey sandwich with white bread (120g), lean ground turkey (130g), lettuce (50g), tomato (60g), and mayo (20g). Side of sweet potato (150g boiled).
    * Protein: 35g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 85g

* **Afternoon Snack (4:00 PM):** Bagel (90g) with coconut butter (20g) and banana slices (100g).
    * Protein: 8g, Fat: 8g, Carbs: 75g

* **Dinner (7:00 PM):** Baked white fish (150g - cod or tilapia), boiled potatoes (200g), steamed green beans (150g), and olive oil (15ml).
    * Protein: 35g, Fat: 20g, Carbs: 70g

* **Evening Snack (9:30 PM):** Rice pudding made with coconut milk (200g) and white rice (50g cooked).
    * Protein: 10g, Fat: 25g, Carbs: 30g

This meal plan prioritizes easily digestible carbohydrates for sustained energy during marathon training, with strategic meal timing to fuel the morning run and support recovery. All foods are nut-free and dairy-free while maintaining optimal macronutrient ratios for endurance performance.
